# Workshop on Simple and Reproducible Deep Learning

This workshop focuses on simple, elegant, and reproducible techniques that improve the
efficiency, robustness, or understanding of deep learning models *without* requiring
large-scale compute. We especially welcome ideas that can be validated on small,
academic-scale datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, MNIST variants, small text corpora) and that
clearly distinguish themselves from existing literature.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
- Data-efficient training and data augmentation
- Regularization and its effect on generalization
- Robustness to label noise and distribution shift
- Optimization dynamics and learning-rate behavior
- Surprising or negative empirical results that challenge common assumptions

A strong submission stems from a simple question or observation, proposes a concrete and
feasible experiment, and reports a clear, measurable finding.
